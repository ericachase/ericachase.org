About

Erica Chase, CPACC

Hi there! My name is Erica Chase and I am a 26-year-old digital accessibility consultant and graphic designer from Chicago, Illinois. I have a Bachelor of Science in Applied Mathematics from Northern Illinois University with minors in Computer Science and Visual Communication.

I have had a variety of roles in the digital space, including software development, interaction design, UIUX design, user research, and accessibility consulting. In addition, I am also a Certified Professional in Accessibility Core Competencies (CPACC) with the International Association of Accessibility Professionals (IAAP), as well as a DHS Trusted Tester with the Department of Homeland Security.

As the IT Accessibility Consultant at Tech for All, I focus on web accessibility testing using code analysis and assistive technologies, primarily voice control software. I also work with a team to lead training sessions on accessible document creation in Microsoft Office applications and PDF, as well as web navigation and testing. Additionally, I assist with project management, including meeting with potential clients, creating project estimates and SOWs, and determining resource allocation.

I live in Wicker Park, Chicago with my 9-month-old black tabby cat, Peach. In my free time, I enjoy knitting, video games, cooking, and reading as much as possible. I also play the violin with the Chicago City Wide Symphony Orchestra.
